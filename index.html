This is how I work. Lots of others also work remotely. Perfect as it means I can live where I want and be fairly flexible about hours - I live in Scotland, in the winter it stays dark till late in the morning and gets dark super early. Being able to get out for a run in the middle of the day is super important to me. Have also worked from other places, a coworking space in Germany, an apartment in Chamonix.

Challenges

Not missing out on things happening in the office. Both social and work related. No 'water cooler' conversations. Still feeling like part of the team. It's easier the more remote people there are on the team.

Champion of remote happiness

At Tes we have a system of "Champions" where one person takes responsibility for a specific aspect of life at Tes. Then we rotate after a few months. We've just started doing this, and my first Champion role was the Champion of remote happiness. Aim is to address any challenges within this area & for the first time of having the role wanted to define some guidelines.

Meeting guidelines

  • Use the snowball microphones if there is more than one person around a computer
    • Each team should have (or be getting) one
  • Check that all participants can hear
    • If you can’t hear, check your speaker volume is turned up. If it is and you still can’t hear, let everyone else in the meeting know!
  • Go on mute if you’re not speaking
    • Especially if you’re in a noisy office. If that’s causing a problem, consider moving somewhere quieter and / or ordering a better headset from Service Now
  • Try to put a camera on the speaker
    • Zoom allows screensharing and video at the same time
  • In a large group Q & A, always repeat the question
    • Chances are not everyone heard the first time
Have shared this with other parts of Tes, eg. HR if they want to run remote meetings - it's only really the engineering team who are remote.
